PHILOSOPHY

The Panorama Montessori School, Inc (PMSI) is an established and organized educational institution, who believes on the following:

We believe that students learn in a safe and nurturing environment that supports the diversity of each student.
We believe that by giving the students different opportunities, they will be able to develop their skills and nurture their relationships with others.
We believe that parent-school collaboration is a powerful tool in helping the children attain their maximum potentials.

CURRICULUM

Pre-School

It refers to the educational program/s preparatory to Grade I, developing the child’s competencies: physical, social, emotional, and cognitive skills so that they become ready for formal schooling.

No better preparation for school life and schoolwork except by giving a child various concrete learning experiences in his/her environment.

  • Kinder 1: 4 years old
  • Kinder 2: 5 years old
  • Subjects: Values Education preparatory to GMRC, Reading and Languages

Elementary

Serves as the “Core” curriculum which may be augmented with additional subjects and/or additional time. Covers the 13 Learning Areas – Reading and Literacy Language, Makabansa (Araling Panlipunan and MAPEH combined), GMRC, Math, Science, English.

Core Learning Areas: English, Math, Science, Filipino, Araling Panlipunan, MAPEH, TLE/HE, GMRC.
MATATAG Curriculum: Phased implementation from S.Y. 2024–2027.

Junior High School

The “Core” curriculum in Secondary Education may be augmented with additional subjects and/or additional time. It covers Filipino, English, Math, Science, Araling Panlipunan, MAPEH, TLE/HE, GMRC.

Additional subjects may include Music, Arts, Physical Education (TLE), Livelihood Education, ICT, Home Economics.
MATATAG Curriculum: Phased implementation from S.Y. 2024–2028.

Special Education (SPED)

Panorama Montessori School offers classes in Special Education (SPED). These provide specialized instruction for children with disabilities such as learning disabilities, hearing impairments, giftedness, emotional and behavioral disorders, physical disabilities, developmental disabilities, and other health impairments.

SPED classes may be provided in inclusion with general education or in separate classrooms/programs, depending on student needs. Students benefit from individualized instruction, specialized strategies, additional support, and other resources.
